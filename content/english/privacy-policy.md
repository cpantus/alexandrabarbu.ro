## 1. Definition of terms

- **Personal data** — any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (name, email, IP address, etc.)
- **Data subject** — the natural person whose personal data is processed (you, the website user)
- **Data controller** — the natural or legal person who determines the purposes and means of processing personal data
- **Processing** — any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, transmission, deletion, etc.)
- **Consent** — freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous agreement of the data subject for data processing
- **Third party** — any natural or legal person other than the data subject, the controller, or the controller's processors

## 4. What information we collect and why

### 4.1. Contact form

When you send us a message through the contact form, we collect:

- **Name** — to address you personally
- **Email address** — to reply to you
- **Your message** — to understand and respond to your request

**Legal basis:** your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), expressed by completing and submitting the form.

**Processing:** Data is transmitted via Google Apps Script (a Google LLC service) and stored in order to respond to your request.

### 4.2. Bookings via Cal.com

When you schedule a consultation through the Cal.com widget integrated on the website, Cal.com collects:

- **Name**
- **Email address**
- **Appointment date and time**

**Legal basis:** performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR).

**Processing:** Data is managed by Cal.com Inc. in accordance with their [privacy policy](https://cal.com/privacy).

### 4.3. ADHD self-assessment test

The ADHD self-assessment test available on the website operates **entirely in your browser** (client-side). The answers to the 35 questions:

- **Are NOT transmitted** to any server
- **Are NOT stored** in any database
- **Are NOT accessible** to us or any third party

The result is calculated locally in your browser and is lost when you close the page. We do not collect any personal data through this test.

### 4.4. Google Search Console (active)

Google Search Console is a tool for monitoring website visibility in the Google search engine. Through this service, Google collects and provides us with:

- Search queries that lead users to the website
- Indexed URLs of the website
- Search performance (click-through rate — CTR, average position, number of impressions)
- Device type used (desktop, mobile, tablet)
- Country from which results are accessed

**Purpose:** Monitoring website visibility in Google search and optimizing content to be easier to find.

**Legal basis:** our legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) in understanding how the website is discovered through searches.

**Note:** This data is aggregated and does not directly identify individual users.

### 4.5. Google Analytics (planned)

Google Analytics is a web analytics service planned for activation on this website. When activated, it will collect:

- IP address (anonymized)
- Pages visited and session duration
- Traffic source (direct, organic, social media)
- Browser type and operating system
- Screen resolution
- Approximate geographic location (city/country)
- Browsing behavior (page visit order, bounce rate)

**Purpose:** Understanding how users interact with the website to improve content and browsing experience.

**Legal basis:** your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), which will be requested before activating analytics cookies.

**Google Analytics cookies:**

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `_ga` | Distinguishes unique users | 2 years |
| `_ga_<ID>` | Maintains session state | 2 years |
| `_gid` | Distinguishes unique users | 24 hours |

**Opt-out:** You can disable Google Analytics by installing the [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

### 4.6. Google reCAPTCHA v3 (active)

The contact form is protected by Google reCAPTCHA v3, a service that distinguishes human users from automated programs (bots). This service collects:

- Page interactions (mouse movements, typing patterns)
- IP address

**Purpose:** Protecting the contact form against spam and automated abuse.

**Legal basis:** our legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) in protecting the website against abusive use.

**Note:** reCAPTCHA v3 operates invisibly, without requiring any interaction on your part.

### 4.7. Automatically collected technical information

When you access the website, hosting servers automatically collect certain technical information:

**Purpose:** Ensuring proper operation and security of the website.

**Legal basis:** our legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) in maintaining website security and functionality.

## 5. Cookies and local storage

### 5.1. Technical cookies

- **reCAPTCHA v3:** Sets cookies necessary for anti-spam protection (including `_GRECAPTCHA` and other associated Google cookies). These are essential for the contact form to function.

### 5.2. Analytics cookies (planned)

When Google Analytics is activated, the following cookies will be set:

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `_ga` | Distinguishes unique users | 2 years |
| `_ga_<ID>` | Maintains session state | 2 years |
| `_gid` | Distinguishes unique users | 24 hours |

These cookies will be activated **only with your consent**.

### 5.3. Cal.com cookies

The Cal.com booking widget may set its own cookies necessary for the booking service to function. These cookies are managed by Cal.com in accordance with their [privacy policy](https://cal.com/privacy).

### 5.4. Local storage (localStorage)

The website uses localStorage (local browser storage) for:

- **`preferredLanguage`** — saves your language preference (Romanian/English) to display the website in your preferred language on future visits
- **`cookie-consent`** — saves the fact that you have seen and accepted the information banner

**Note:** localStorage is not a cookie. Data is stored exclusively in your browser, on your device, and is not transmitted to any server. You can delete this data at any time from your browser settings.

### 5.5. How to disable cookies

You can control and delete cookies from your browser settings:

- **Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
- **Firefox:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
- **Safari:** Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
- **Edge:** Settings → Cookies and site permissions

**Warning:** Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some website elements (for example, the anti-spam protection on the contact form).
