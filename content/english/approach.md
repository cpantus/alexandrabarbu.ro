# Method Tabs Section - Main Therapeutic Methods
method_tabs:
  title: "**Evidence-Based** Therapeutic Methods"
  description: "I use a flexible combination of scientifically validated methods, adapted to your specific needs"
  methods:
    - name: "Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)"
      slug: "cbt"
      icon: "brain"
      description: |
        CBT is a structured, solution-oriented method that helps you identify and modify
        thoughts and behaviors that contribute to your difficulties. It is effective for anxiety,
        depression, panic attacks, and many other challenges.
      benefits:
        - "Demonstrable results in 12-20 sessions"
        - "Practical tools you can use daily"
        - "Focus on present and solving current problems"
        - "Scientifically proven efficacy for many conditions"
    - name: "Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)"
      slug: "eft"
      icon: "heart"
      description: |
        EFT helps you understand and process your deep emotions, often rooted in early
        attachment relationships. This method is particularly useful for relational difficulties,
        attachment issues and healing emotional trauma.
      benefits:
        - "Deep processing of emotions and needs"
        - "Improving intimate relationships and secure attachment"
        - "Emotional healing and increased self-awareness"
        - "Effective for couples and individual therapy"
    - name: "Solution-Focused Therapy (SFT)"
      slug: "sft"
      icon: "lightbulb"
      description: |
        SFT emphasizes your resources, your strengths and the future you want, rather
        than past problems. It is a pragmatic approach, oriented toward concrete goals and rapid changes.
      benefits:
        - "Focus on solutions, not problems"
        - "Quick results (often in 5-8 sessions)"
        - "Using your resources and strengths"
        - "Ideal for clear and specific goals"
    - name: "Mindfulness and ACT"
      slug: "mindfulness-act"
      icon: "spa"
      description: |
        Mindfulness and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) teach you to be present in the moment,
        accept difficult experiences and act according to your values. These methods are
        excellent for reducing stress, anxiety and increasing general wellbeing.
      benefits:
        - "Reducing stress and anxiety"
        - "Increasing self-awareness and presence"
        - "Accepting difficult experiences without avoidance"
        - "Clarifying values and value-driven action"
